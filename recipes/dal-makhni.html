Dal Makhni

Description

Dal Makhni is a luxurious lentil dish from North India, known for its rich, creamy texture and deep, comforting flavors. Made with whole black lentils (urad dal) and red kidney beans, it's slow-cooked for hours with butter, cream, tomatoes, and a delicate blend of spices like cumin, cloves, and kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves). This slow cooking gives the dal a velvety consistency and allows the flavors to fully develop.

Traditionally served with naan, roti, or steamed basmati rice, Dal Makhni is a staple at festive occasions, family gatherings, and Indian restaurants around the world. Its balance of smoky, buttery, and mildly spiced notes makes it a timeless favorite—comforting to locals and a flavorful introduction to Indian cuisine for international food lovers.

Ingredients Required:-

  • 3/4 cup whole black lentils (urad dal)
  • 1/4 cup red kidney beans
  • 1 large onion | finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes | pureed
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • 1 tsp kasuri methi (optional)
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Steps to follow :-

  1. Soak and Cook Lentils: Soak urad dal and rajma overnight. Pressure cook with 4 cups water until soft (25 to 30 mins).

  2. Soak and Cook Lentils: In a heavy-bottomed pan, melt butter. Add cumin, onions, and sauté until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until aromatic.

  3. Add Tomatoes & Spices: Stir in tomato puree, turmeric, chili powder, and salt. Cook until oil separates.

  4. Combine and Simmer: Add cooked lentils and 1 to 2 cups water. Simmer on low heat for 30 to 40 mins, stirring occasionally.

  5. Finish Creamy: Stir in cream, garam masala, and kasuri methi. Simmer another 5 to 10 minutes.

  6. Serve: Garnish with fresh coriander and a swirl of cream. Serve hot with naan or rice.
